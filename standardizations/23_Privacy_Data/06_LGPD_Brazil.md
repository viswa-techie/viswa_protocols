# LGPD — Brazil's General Data Protection Law

**Topic:** Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados Pessoais (LGPD) — Brazil's General Data Protection Law  
**Law:** Lei nº 13.709/2018 (amended by Lei nº 13.853/2019)  
**Enforcement Authority:** ANPD (Autoridade Nacional de Proteção de Dados)  
**Effective:** September 18, 2020 (substantive provisions); August 1, 2021 (administrative sanctions)  
**Domain:** Personal data protection; Brazil; Latin America  
**Audience:** DPOs/Encarregados, privacy counsel, compliance officers, Brazilian market businesses  
**Prerequisites:** Basic privacy law concepts; understanding of GDPR (for comparison); knowledge of Brazilian legal system helpful

## Chapter 1 — Historical Context & Origin Story

### 1.1 Timeline

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 1988 | Brazilian Federal Constitution — Art. 5 (right to privacy; habeas data) |
| 2011 | Marco Civil da Internet (Internet Bill of Rights — Lei 12.965/2014; discussions begin) |
| 2014 | Marco Civil da Internet enacted (net neutrality; privacy provisions; but no comprehensive data protection) |
| 2016 | EU GDPR adopted — creates urgency for Brazilian equivalent |
| 2018 | **LGPD signed** (Lei 13.709, August 14, 2018) — comprehensive personal data protection |
| 2019 | Lei 13.853/2019 — creates ANPD (Autoridade Nacional de Proteção de Dados); amends LGPD; establishes DPO role |
| 2020 | **LGPD takes effect** (September 18, 2020) — after pandemic-related delays |
| 2021 | **Administrative sanctions** in force (August 1, 2021) — ANPD can impose fines |
| 2022 | Constitutional Amendment 115 — elevates data protection to FUNDAMENTAL RIGHT (Art. 5, LXXIX) |
| 2022 | ANPD issues first regulations: security incidents; international transfers; DPO role |
| 2023 | ANPD issues first administrative sanctions; begins enforcement activity; Regulation on international transfers published |
| 2024 | ANPD recognized as independent body (Lei 14.460/2022 implemented); expanded enforcement; adequacy discussions with EU |

### 1.2 Key Influences

| Source | Influence on LGPD |
|:------:|---|
| **EU GDPR** | Primary model: consent framework; legal bases; data subject rights; DPO; DPIA; international transfers |
| **Brazilian Constitution** | Art. 5 (personality rights; privacy; intimacy); habeas data writ; Constitutional Amendment 115 (2022) |
| **Marco Civil (2014)** | Internet-specific provisions; logging requirements; judicial access; consent for data collection online |
| **Consumer Defense Code (CDC)** | Consumer rights framework; transparency; vulnerability of consumers |
| **OECD Privacy Guidelines** | Principles-based approach; collection limitation; purpose specification |

## Chapter 2 — LGPD Architecture & Structure

### 2.1 Document Structure

| Chapter | Articles | Content |
|:-------:|:--------:|---------|
| I | Art. 1-6 | Preliminary provisions (scope; definitions; principles) |
| II | Art. 7-16 | Processing of personal data (legal bases; consent; sensitive data; children) |
| III | Art. 17-22 | Rights of the data subject (titular) |
| IV | Art. 23-32 | Processing by public sector |
| V | Art. 33-36 | International transfer of personal data |
| VI | Art. 37-45 | Processing agents (controller; operator; DPO) |
| VII | Art. 46-49 | Security and good practices |
| VIII | Art. 50 | Governance and good practices (codes of conduct) |
| IX | Art. 52-54 | Administrative sanctions |
| X | Art. 55-A–59 | ANPD (authority structure; powers) |

### 2.2 Key Definitions (Art. 5)

| Term (Portuguese) | English | Definition |
|:---:|:---:|---|
| **Dado pessoal** | Personal data | Information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person |
| **Dado pessoal sensível** | Sensitive personal data | Racial/ethnic origin; religious belief; political opinion; trade union; religious/philosophical/political organization; health; sex life; genetic; biometric (when linked to natural person) |
| **Titular** | Data subject | Natural person to whom the personal data relates |
| **Controlador** | Controller | Natural or legal person that makes decisions regarding the processing of personal data |
| **Operador** | Operator (Processor) | Natural or legal person that processes personal data on behalf of the controller |
| **Encarregado** | DPO | Person appointed to act as communication channel between controller, data subjects, and ANPD |
| **Tratamento** | Processing | Any operation performed on personal data (collection; production; reception; classification; use; access; storage; evaluation; transfer; deletion; etc.) |
| **Consentimento** | Consent | Free, informed, and unambiguous manifestation by which data subject agrees to processing for a defined purpose |
| **Anonimização** | Anonymization | Use of reasonable technical means to make data unable to be associated with an individual (directly or indirectly) |

### 2.3 Principles (Art. 6)

| # | Principle (PT) | English | Meaning |
|:-:|:---:|:---:|---|
| I | Finalidade | Purpose | Processing for legitimate, specific, explicit purposes communicated to data subject |
| II | Adequação | Adequacy | Processing compatible with communicated purposes |
| III | Necessidade | Necessity | Limited to minimum necessary for stated purpose (data minimization) |
| IV | Livre acesso | Free access | Easy, free access for data subjects to know about processing |
| V | Qualidade dos dados | Data quality | Accuracy; clarity; relevance; updated |
| VI | Transparência | Transparency | Clear; precise; easily accessible information about processing |
| VII | Segurança | Security | Technical and organizational measures to protect data |
| VIII | Prevenção | Prevention | Measures to prevent harm from processing |
| IX | Não discriminação | Non-discrimination | No processing for discriminatory, unlawful, or abusive purposes |
| X | Responsabilização e prestação de contas | Accountability | Demonstrate effective measures; prove compliance |

## Chapter 3 — Legal Bases (Art. 7)

### 3.1 Ten Legal Bases for Processing Personal Data

| # | Legal Basis | Description | GDPR Equivalent |
|:-:|:---:|---|:---:|
| I | **Consent** | Free, informed, unambiguous consent for specific purpose | Art. 6(1)(a) |
| II | **Legal obligation** | Compliance with legal or regulatory obligation | Art. 6(1)(c) |
| III | **Public administration** | Processing by public bodies for public policies | Art. 6(1)(e) (partially) |
| IV | **Research** | By research bodies; anonymization preferred | — (no direct equivalent) |
| V | **Contract** | Performance of contract or preliminary procedures | Art. 6(1)(b) |
| VI | **Exercise of rights** | Regular exercise of rights in judicial/administrative/arbitration proceedings | — (no direct equivalent) |
| VII | **Life protection** | Protection of life or physical safety | Art. 6(1)(d) (vital interests) |
| VIII | **Health protection** | Health procedures by professionals/health services | — (specific to health) |
| IX | **Legitimate interests** | Legitimate interests of controller or third party (unless overridden by data subject rights) | Art. 6(1)(f) |
| X | **Credit protection** | Credit protection (debt collection; credit scoring) | — (Brazil-specific) |

### 3.2 Sensitive Data Processing (Art. 11)

| Legal Basis for Sensitive Data | Detail |
|:---:|---|
| **Explicit consent** | Specific and highlighted consent for sensitive data processing |
| **Without consent (Art. 11(II))** | Only when INDISPENSABLE for: legal obligation; public policy (by public bodies); research (anonymized when possible); life/physical safety protection; health (by health professionals/services); fraud prevention |

**Key difference from GDPR:** LGPD has NO explicit consent exception for "legitimate interests" for sensitive data. Sensitive data processing without consent is restricted to specific enumerated purposes.

### 3.3 Children's Data (Art. 14)

| Requirement | Detail |
|:-----------:|--------|
| **Consent** | Must obtain specific, highlighted consent from at least one parent/legal guardian |
| **Best interest** | Processing must be in the best interest of the child |
| **No conditioning** | Cannot condition participation in games/apps on disclosure of more data than necessary |
| **Transparency** | Information about processing must be clear, accessible, and adapted to child's understanding |

## Chapter 4 — Data Subject Rights (Art. 17-22)

### 4.1 Rights of the Titular

| Right | Article | Description |
|:-----:|:-------:|-------------|
| **Confirmation** | Art. 18(I) | Confirm that processing of their data takes place |
| **Access** | Art. 18(II) | Access to their personal data |
| **Correction** | Art. 18(III) | Correct incomplete, inaccurate, or outdated data |
| **Anonymization/blocking/deletion** | Art. 18(IV) | Of excessive or unnecessary data, or data processed in violation of LGPD |
| **Portability** | Art. 18(V) | Transfer data to another service/product provider (per ANPD regulation) |
| **Deletion** | Art. 18(VI) | Delete personal data processed with consent (when consent withdrawn) |
| **Information about sharing** | Art. 18(VII) | Know which entities (public/private) data was shared with |
| **Information about non-consent** | Art. 18(VIII) | Be informed about possibility of not consenting and consequences |
| **Consent withdrawal** | Art. 18(IX) | Revoke consent (simple; free procedure) |
| **Review of automated decisions** | Art. 20 | Request review of decisions made solely by automated processing |
| **Opposition** | Art. 18(§2) | Oppose processing when not based on consent (if non-compliant with LGPD) |

### 4.2 Automated Decision-Making (Art. 20)

| Aspect | LGPD Provision |
|:------:|----------------|
| **Right to review** | Data subject may request review of decisions made solely based on automated processing |
| **Information** | Controller shall provide clear, adequate information about criteria and procedures used |
| **Audit** | ANPD may conduct audit to verify discriminatory aspects in automated processing |
| **Limitation** | May not disclose trade secrets, but must provide sufficient information for data subject to understand decision logic |

## Chapter 5 — International Data Transfers (Art. 33-36)

### 5.1 Transfer Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Article | Description |
|:---------:|:-------:|-------------|
| **Adequate protection** | Art. 33(I) | Countries/organizations recognized by ANPD as providing adequate level of protection |
| **Contractual guarantees** | Art. 33(II) | Standard contractual clauses; specific clauses; global corporate rules |
| **BCRs (Normas corporativas globais)** | Art. 33(II)(b) | Binding corporate rules approved by ANPD |
| **International cooperation** | Art. 33(III) | International legal cooperation agreements |
| **Authorization by ANPD** | Art. 33(IV) | ANPD-granted authorization |
| **Consent** | Art. 33(VIII) | Specific, informed consent for transfer (with prior information about international nature) |
| **Legal obligation/public interest** | Art. 33(VI,VII) | Legal obligation or public policy requires transfer |
| **Life protection** | Art. 33(V) | Necessary for life or physical safety of data subject or third party |
| **Contract performance** | Art. 33(IX) | Transfer necessary for contract performance (at data subject's request) |

## Chapter 6 — ANPD Enforcement & Sanctions

### 6.1 Administrative Sanctions (Art. 52)

| Sanction | Detail |
|:--------:|--------|
| **Warning** | With deadline for corrective measures |
| **Simple fine** | Up to 2% of revenue in Brazil (per legal entity, group, or conglomerate); maximum R$50 million per infraction |
| **Daily fine** | Per day of non-compliance; subject to total maximum of R$50 million |
| **Publicization** | Publication of the infraction after confirmed |
| **Blocking** | Blocking of personal data subject to infraction (until regularized) |
| **Deletion** | Deletion of personal data subject to infraction |
| **Partial suspension** | Suspension of database processing for up to 6 months (extendable) |
| **Total suspension** | Suspension of entire processing activity for up to 6 months |
| **Prohibition** | Partial or total prohibition of processing activity |

### 6.2 Comparison of Fines

| Jurisdiction | Maximum Fine |
|:---:|:---:|
| LGPD (Brazil) | 2% of revenue in Brazil; max R$50M (~$10M USD) per infraction |
| GDPR (EU) | 4% of global annual turnover; max €20M (whichever is higher) |
| CCPA/CPRA (US-CA) | $2,500-$7,500 per violation |

### 6.3 Notable Enforcement Actions

| Year | Target | Sanction | Violation |
|:----:|:------:|:--------:|-----------|
| 2023 | Telecom company | Warning + daily fine | Data breach; failure to notify ANPD and data subjects within reasonable time |
| 2023 | Government body | Warning + corrective measures | Processing without adequate legal basis; lack of transparency in notice |
| 2024 | E-commerce platform | Under investigation | Excessive data collection; cookie consent issues; inadequate privacy notice |

## Chapter 7 — LGPD vs. GDPR Comparison

| Aspect | LGPD (Brazil) | GDPR (EU) |
|:------:|:---:|:---:|
| **Legal bases** | 10 (includes credit protection + exercise of rights) | 6 |
| **Sensitive data bases** | Explicit consent or enumerated exceptions (narrower) | Explicit consent + 9 exceptions (broader) |
| **DPO requirement** | Required for ALL controllers (Encarregado); ANPD may regulate exceptions | Required in specific circumstances (public body; core activity monitoring/sensitive) |
| **Fines** | 2% revenue in Brazil; max R$50M | 4% global revenue; max €20M |
| **Breach notification** | "Reasonable time" to ANPD (ANPD regulation specifies: 3 working days) | 72 hours to DPA |
| **International transfers** | 9 mechanisms; adequacy decisions pending | SCCs; BCRs; adequacy; derogations |
| **Private right of action** | Yes (civil liability; consumer code) | Yes (Art. 82; right to compensation) |
| **Legitimate interest** | Requires LIA (Legitimate Interest Assessment) — similar to balancing test | Balancing test; DPIA when high risk |
| **Children** | Specific consent from parent/guardian | Specific provisions (Art. 8); age varies by member state (13-16) |
| **Enforcement body** | ANPD (single federal authority) | National DPAs (per member state) |
| **Constitutional status** | Fundamental right (CE 115/2022) | Charter of Fundamental Rights (Art. 8) |
| **Class actions** | Yes (strong collective action tradition in Brazil) | Limited (representative actions from 2023) |

## Chapter 9 — Case Studies

### 9.1 Multinational Operating in Brazil: LGPD Implementation

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Organization** | US technology company; Brazilian subsidiary (LTDA); 2M Brazilian users; B2C app + B2B SaaS |
| **Pre-LGPD state** | Had GDPR program for EU users; Brazilian operations treated as "low regulation" market |
| **Challenge** | (1) 10 legal bases (different from GDPR's 6). (2) Encarregado required (not just "sometimes" like GDPR DPO). (3) "Credit protection" legal basis (Brazil-specific; needed for fintech feature). (4) Portuguese-language compliance (privacy notice; consent forms; internal documentation). |
| **Implementation** | |

| Workstream | Actions |
|:---:|---|
| **Governance** | Appointed local Encarregado (Brazilian privacy professional; Portuguese-speaking). ANPD registration of controller. Board-level privacy reporting. |
| **Legal basis mapping** | Reviewed all processing activities. For each: mapped to LGPD Art. 7 legal basis (not just GDPR bases). Discovered: (a) credit scoring → use Art. 7(X) credit protection; (b) marketing → consent (Art. 7(I)); (c) contract performance → Art. 7(V); (d) legitimate interest for fraud prevention → Art. 7(IX) with LIA. |
| **Rights** | Adapted DSR portal for Brazilian requirements. Key differences: (a) 15-day response time (not 30 days). (b) Right to "information about sharing" (Art. 18(VII)) — needed new reporting. (c) Portability format per ANPD regulation. |
| **International transfers** | Data processed in US. Implemented: (a) ANPD standard contractual clauses. (b) User consent for transfer (with specific disclosure about US processing). |
| **Language** | All user-facing materials in Brazilian Portuguese. Internal privacy procedures: Portuguese + English. Training materials: Portuguese. |

**Outcome:** Fully compliant by enforcement date. Zero ANPD inquiries. Competitive advantage (trusted brand in Brazilian market; B2B customers require LGPD compliance evidence).

### 9.2 Brazilian Bank: Legitimate Interest Assessment

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Scenario** | Brazilian bank wants to use transaction data to detect fraud (not based on consent; customers didn't explicitly consent to fraud analysis) |
| **Legal basis** | Art. 7(IX) — Legitimate interests of controller |
| **LIA (Relatório de Impacto)** | Required assessment: |

| LIA Step | Analysis |
|:---:|---|
| **Legitimate interest identified** | Fraud detection and prevention; regulatory obligation (Bacen requirements); protecting customers from unauthorized transactions |
| **Necessity** | Transaction monitoring is ONLY effective way to detect fraud in real-time; cannot rely on consent (would alert fraudsters); alternatives insufficient |
| **Balancing test** | Titular's expectation: customers EXPECT bank to protect their accounts. Impact on titular: minimal (no new data collected; analysis of existing transaction data). Safeguards: (a) only automated alerts trigger human review; (b) no data shared externally; (c) retention limited to 5 years (regulatory requirement); (d) opt-out of marketing derived from fraud patterns |
| **Conclusion** | Legitimate interest applies; no override by titular's rights; safeguards adequate |
| **ANPD documentation** | LIA documented; available for ANPD inspection; referenced in ROPA (Records of Processing Activities) |

## Chapter 11 — Interview Questions & Career Guide

### Tier 1: Entry-Level

**Q1:** What are the 10 legal bases under LGPD Art. 7, and which two are unique to Brazil (not found in GDPR)?

**A:**

| # | Legal Basis | Unique to Brazil? |
|:-:|:---:|:---:|
| I | Consent | No (GDPR Art. 6(1)(a)) |
| II | Legal obligation | No (GDPR Art. 6(1)(c)) |
| III | Public administration | Partially (GDPR Art. 6(1)(e) similar) |
| IV | Research | **Yes** — specific legal basis for research bodies |
| V | Contract | No (GDPR Art. 6(1)(b)) |
| VI | Exercise of rights | **Yes** — judicial/administrative/arbitration proceedings |
| VII | Life protection | No (GDPR Art. 6(1)(d) vital interests) |
| VIII | Health protection | Partially (GDPR uses vital interests + public health) |
| IX | Legitimate interests | No (GDPR Art. 6(1)(f)) |
| X | Credit protection | **Yes** — Brazil-specific (credit scoring; debt collection) |

**Brazil-unique legal bases:**
1. **Exercise of rights (VI):** Allows processing necessary for exercising rights in judicial, administrative, or arbitration proceedings — reflecting Brazil's litigious environment.
2. **Credit protection (X):** Reflects importance of credit bureaus (SPC/Serasa) in Brazilian commerce; allows processing for credit scoring without consent.

### Tier 2: Mid-Level

**Q2:** Compare the role of the Encarregado (DPO) under LGPD with the DPO under GDPR.

**A:**

| Aspect | LGPD Encarregado | GDPR DPO |
|:------:|:---:|:---:|
| **When required** | ALL controllers (Art. 41) — ANPD may simplify for SMEs | Only when: public body; core activity = large-scale monitoring/sensitive data |
| **Appointed by** | Controller (Art. 41) | Controller OR processor |
| **Independence** | Not explicitly guaranteed in LGPD (ANPD regulation may clarify) | Explicitly protected (Art. 38: no instructions; no dismissal for performing tasks) |
| **Qualifications** | Not specified by law (ANPD may regulate) | Expert knowledge of data protection law and practices (Art. 37(5)) |
| **Tasks** | (a) Accept complaints from titulares. (b) Receive ANPD communications. (c) Guide employees. (d) Execute other tasks defined by controller/ANPD | (a) Inform/advise. (b) Monitor compliance. (c) Advise on DPIA. (d) Cooperate with DPA. (e) Contact point for DPA |
| **Conflict of interest** | Not explicitly addressed | Cannot hold position creating conflict of interest (Art. 38(6)) |
| **External** | May be person or organizational unit; external allowed | May be internal employee or external service provider (Art. 37(6)) |
| **Publication** | Identity and contact must be published (website) | Contact details communicated to supervisory authority + published |

### Tier 3: Senior

**Q3:** A Brazilian fintech wants to implement an AI-based credit scoring system. Analyze the LGPD implications including legal basis, automated decision-making rights, non-discrimination principle, and ANPD compliance requirements.

**A:**

| Aspect | Analysis |
|:------:|---------|
| **Legal basis** | Art. 7(X) — Credit protection: specifically designed for credit scoring. No consent needed. BUT: must still comply with all other principles (especially non-discrimination). |
| **Art. 20 (Automated decisions)** | Titular has right to REQUEST REVIEW of automated credit decision. Controller MUST: (a) provide clear information about criteria/procedures used in scoring; (b) allow human review upon request; (c) not disclose trade secrets BUT still explain logic sufficiently. |
| **Non-discrimination (Art. 6(IX))** | Scoring algorithm CANNOT discriminate on: race/ethnicity; religion; political opinion; health; sex life. Implementation: (a) remove protected attributes from features; (b) test for proxy discrimination (zip code → race); (c) conduct fairness audits (demographic parity; equalized odds); (d) document compliance. |
| **RIPD (DPIA equivalent)** | Required: processing creates significant risk to titulares' rights (credit decisions affect financial access). Must document: (a) types of data processed; (b) methodology; (c) risk mitigation; (d) safeguards. |
| **Transparency** | Art. 9 + Art. 20: (a) inform titular that automated scoring is used; (b) explain which data points influence score; (c) provide score upon request (Art. 18(II) — access); (d) explain how to improve score. |
| **Minimum data** | Art. 6(III) — Necessity: only process data actually predictive of creditworthiness. Cannot use: social media posts; contacts list; arbitrary behavioral data. Must justify each data input's relevance. |
| **Retention** | Score may be retained while credit relationship exists. Historical scoring data: per Banco Central regulations (5 years). Data used for scoring: only while relevant to current assessment. |
| **ANPD audit risk** | ANPD may audit for discrimination (Art. 20, §2). Must maintain: model documentation; training data records; fairness metrics; bias testing results; human review logs. |

  • Blocking/deletion of data
  • Suspension of processing (6 months)
  • Prohibition of processing

═══════════════════════════════════════════
KEY ROLES:
  Titular = Data Subject
  Controlador = Controller
  Operador = Processor
  Encarregado = DPO (required for ALL controllers)

═══════════════════════════════════════════
INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS (Art. 33):
  • Adequacy decisions (pending from ANPD)
  • Standard Contractual Clauses (ANPD model 2024)
  • BCRs (regulation pending)
  • Specific consent
  • Legal obligation / public interest
  • Contract performance

═══════════════════════════════════════════
KEY DIFFERENCES FROM GDPR:
  • 10 legal bases (vs. 6)
  • Fines: 2% Brazil revenue / R$50M (vs. 4% global / €20M)
  • DPO: required for ALL controllers (vs. specific cases)
  • Breach notification: 3 working days (regulation)
  • Credit protection: specific legal basis
  • No "legitimate interest" for sensitive data
  • Children: parental consent mandatory (Art. 14)
