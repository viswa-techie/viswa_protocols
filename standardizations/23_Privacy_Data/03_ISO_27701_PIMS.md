# ISO/IEC 27701 — Privacy Information Management System (PIMS)

**Topic:** ISO/IEC 27701:2019 — Extension to ISO/IEC 27001 and ISO/IEC 27002 for Privacy Information Management  
**Standard:** ISO/IEC 27701:2019  
**SDO:** ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 27 (Information security, cybersecurity and privacy protection)  
**Domain:** Privacy management systems; PII protection; GDPR compliance certification  
**Audience:** Information Security/Privacy professionals, DPOs, ISMS auditors, compliance managers  
**Prerequisites:** ISO/IEC 27001 knowledge (ISMS); GDPR or privacy law awareness; risk management concepts

---

## Chapter 1 — Historical Context & Origin Story

### 1.1 Timeline

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 2005 | ISO/IEC 27001:2005 — Information Security Management System (ISMS) |
| 2011 | ISO/IEC 29100 — Privacy Framework (principles for privacy) |
| 2013 | ISO/IEC 27001:2013 — Updated ISMS standard |
| 2017 | ISO/IEC 29151 — Code of Practice for PII Protection |
| 2017 | Development of ISO/IEC 27552 begins (later renumbered to 27701) |
| 2018 | GDPR enforcement — creates urgent market demand for privacy certification |
| 2019 | **ISO/IEC 27701:2019 published** (August) — Privacy Information Management System |
| 2020 | First organizations achieve ISO 27701 certification; CNIL (France) recognizes as GDPR compliance tool |
| 2022 | ISO/IEC 27001:2022 published (updated); ISO 27701 alignment with new 27001 |
| 2023 | Wide adoption: 5,000+ organizations certified; recognized by DPAs as accountability evidence |
| 2024 | Revision discussions: aligning with evolving privacy laws (CPRA, PIPL, DPDPA); potential ISO 27701:202x |

### 1.2 Why ISO 27701?

| Problem | Solution via ISO 27701 |
|:-------:|---|
| GDPR Art. 5(2) requires "accountability" — demonstrate compliance | ISO 27701 provides SYSTEMATIC framework to demonstrate privacy compliance |
| Organizations already have ISO 27001 (security) but lack privacy extension | ISO 27701 EXTENDS existing 27001 ISMS — not a separate system |
| Multiple privacy laws require similar controls (GDPR, CCPA, LGPD, etc.) | ISO 27701 maps to MULTIPLE regulations — single system for global compliance |
| Customers/partners need assurance of privacy practices | ISO 27701 CERTIFICATION provides third-party verified assurance |
| Privacy controls are ad-hoc; no systematic management | ISO 27701 provides PDCA cycle (Plan-Do-Check-Act) for privacy management |

## Chapter 3 — Technical Deep Dive: PIMS Controls

### 3.1 Clause 5 — Extensions to ISO 27001

| ISO 27001 Clause | ISO 27701 Extension |
|:---:|---|
| **4. Context** | Identify: role (controller/processor/both); applicable privacy legislation; PII processing scope; interested parties with privacy requirements |
| **5. Leadership** | Top management commitment includes privacy; policy covers privacy; privacy responsibilities assigned |
| **6. Planning** | Risk assessment includes privacy risks (to PII principals); privacy objectives established |
| **7. Support** | Competence includes privacy expertise; awareness includes privacy obligations; documented privacy information |
| **8. Operation** | Privacy risk treatment plans; PII processing documented; privacy by design in operational planning |
| **9. Performance** | Internal audit includes privacy controls; management review includes privacy performance |
| **10. Improvement** | Nonconformities include privacy incidents; continual improvement includes privacy |

### 3.2 Annex A — Controller Controls (31 controls)

| Control Group | Controls | Example Requirements |
|:---:|:---:|---|
| **A.7.2 Conditions for collection** | A.7.2.1-A.7.2.8 | Identify purpose; determine legal basis; consent (when applicable); DPIA; contracts with processors; joint controllers; records of processing |
| **A.7.3 Obligations to PII principals** | A.7.3.1-A.7.3.10 | Information provision (notice); access; correction; erasure; objection; portability; automated decisions; notification of changes |
| **A.7.4 Privacy by design** | A.7.4.1-A.7.4.9 | Limit collection; limit processing; accuracy; minimization; de-identification; deletion at end; temporary files; retention; transmission controls |
| **A.7.5 PII sharing, transfer, disclosure** | A.7.5.1-A.7.5.4 | Identify jurisdictions; identify disclosure; record disclosure; record parties |

### 3.3 Annex B — Processor Controls (18 controls)

| Control Group | Controls | Key Requirements |
|:---:|:---:|---|
| **B.8.2 Conditions for processing** | B.8.2.1-B.8.2.6 | Customer agreement/contract; purpose limitation; marketing restrictions; infringing instructions; obligations to individuals; records of processing |
| **B.8.3 Obligations to PII principals** | B.8.3.1 | Obligations to PII principals (assist controller) |
| **B.8.4 Privacy by design** | B.8.4.1-B.8.4.3 | Temporary files; return/transfer/disposal of PII; PII transmission controls |
| **B.8.5 PII sharing, transfer, disclosure** | B.8.5.1-B.8.5.8 | Identify jurisdictions; countries; records of disclosure; notification of requests; legally binding disclosure; sub-processor disclosure; engagement of sub-processor; change of sub-processor |

## Chapter 4 — Implementation Guide

### 4.1 Implementation Roadmap (for organizations WITH existing ISO 27001)

| Phase | Duration | Activities |
|:-----:|:--------:|:---|
| **1. Gap Analysis** | 2-4 weeks | Compare existing ISMS against ISO 27701 requirements; identify missing controls; assess controller/processor role; identify PII processing scope |
| **2. Scope Extension** | 2-4 weeks | Extend ISMS scope to include PII processing; define PIMS boundaries; identify applicable privacy legislation |
| **3. Privacy Risk Assessment** | 4-6 weeks | Conduct PII-focused risk assessment; identify risks to PII principals (not just organization); evaluate likelihood × impact on individuals |
| **4. Control Implementation** | 8-16 weeks | Implement Clause 6 extensions; implement Annex A (controller) and/or Annex B (processor) controls; document privacy procedures |
| **5. Documentation** | 4-8 weeks | Privacy policy; ROPA; consent procedures; DSR procedures; breach procedures; DPIA procedures; data processing agreements |
| **6. Training & Awareness** | 2-4 weeks | Privacy training for all staff; role-specific training (developers; HR; marketing); DPO/privacy team specialist training |
| **7. Internal Audit** | 2-4 weeks | Audit PIMS against ISO 27701; identify nonconformities; corrective actions |
| **8. Certification Audit** | 2-4 weeks | Stage 1 (documentation review) + Stage 2 (implementation verification); certification decision |

### 4.2 For Organizations WITHOUT ISO 27001

| Approach | Description |
|:--------:|-------------|
| **Integrated implementation** | Implement ISO 27001 + ISO 27701 simultaneously (one project); achieves both certifications |
| **Duration** | Typically 12-18 months (from scratch); 6-12 months if basic security controls already exist |
| **Advantage** | Privacy requirements built-in from the start; no retrofitting |
| **Certification** | Single audit covers both; ISO 27701 certificate references ISO 27001 (cannot have 27701 without 27001) |

### 4.3 Mapping to GDPR (Annex D Summary)

| GDPR Article | ISO 27701 Control |
|:---:|---|
| Art. 5 (Principles) | A.7.4 (PbD); policy; purpose limitation controls |
| Art. 6 (Legal bases) | A.7.2.2 (Identify legal basis) |
| Art. 7 (Consent) | A.7.2.3 (Determine consent); A.7.2.4 (Obtain consent) |
| Art. 13/14 (Information) | A.7.3.2 (Determine information for PII principals) |
| Art. 15-22 (Rights) | A.7.3.3-A.7.3.9 (individual rights controls) |
| Art. 25 (PbD/Default) | A.7.4.1-A.7.4.5 (privacy by design controls) |
| Art. 28 (Processor) | B.8.2 (Conditions for processing); B.8.5 (sub-processors) |
| Art. 30 (Records) | A.7.2.8 / B.8.2.6 (Records of processing) |
| Art. 32 (Security) | ISO 27001 controls + 27002 controls (inherited) |
| Art. 33/34 (Breach) | A.7.3.10 (notification to PII principals) + incident management |
| Art. 35 (DPIA) | A.7.2.5 (Privacy Impact Assessment) |
| Art. 44-49 (Transfers) | A.7.5 / B.8.5 (sharing/transfer/disclosure controls) |

## Chapter 5 — Certification & Audit

### 5.1 Certification Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
|:-----------:|--------|
| **Prerequisite** | Must have ISO/IEC 27001 certification (or achieve simultaneously) |
| **Scope** | PIMS scope must align with ISMS scope; clearly state: controller; processor; or both |
| **Certification body** | Accredited by national accreditation body (UKAS, DAkkS, etc.) for ISO 27701 |
| **Audit stages** | Stage 1: Documentation review (PIMS documentation adequate?). Stage 2: Implementation audit (controls operating effectively?) |
| **Surveillance** | Annual surveillance audits (years 1 and 2); re-certification every 3 years |
| **Certificate** | States: organization name; scope; role (controller/processor); date; accreditation |

### 5.2 Audit Evidence

| ISO 27701 Area | Evidence Required |
|:-:|---|
| **PII inventory** | Records of PII categories; data flows; retention periods; legal bases |
| **Risk assessment** | Privacy risk assessment records; risk to PII principals evaluated; treatment plans |
| **Consent management** | Consent collection mechanisms; withdrawal records; consent audit trail |
| **Data subject rights** | DSR procedures; response records; completion within regulatory deadlines |
| **Breach management** | Incident response procedure; breach log; notification records; lessons learned |
| **Third-party management** | DPAs (Data Processing Agreements); sub-processor management; due diligence records |
| **Training** | Training records; competence assessments; awareness materials |
| **Internal audit** | Audit plans; audit reports; nonconformity tracking; corrective actions |
| **Management review** | Meeting minutes; privacy performance review; improvement decisions |

### 5.3 Common Nonconformities

| Finding | Frequency | Fix |
|:-------:|:---------:|-----|
| Incomplete PII inventory (processing activities not all documented) | High | Comprehensive data mapping exercise; involve all departments |
| Privacy risk assessment doesn't assess risk to INDIVIDUALS (only to organization) | High | Re-frame risk: "what harm to data subjects if this risk materializes?" |
| Consent mechanisms don't meet GDPR requirements (pre-ticked; bundled) | Medium | Redesign consent UX; granular; affirmative; separable |
| DSR procedures exist but not tested/exercised | Medium | Conduct mock DSR exercises; document results |
| Sub-processor management incomplete | High | Inventory all sub-processors; DPAs in place; due diligence documented |
| DPIA not conducted for high-risk processing | Medium | Identify triggers; conduct DPIAs before new processing; document |

|:---------:|:---------:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|

## Chapter 9 — Case Studies

### 9.1 Cloud Service Provider: ISO 27701 Certification (Processor)

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Organization** | SaaS provider; processes data on behalf of 500+ enterprise customers (controller); EU + US operations |
| **Motivation** | (1) Customers demanding GDPR compliance evidence. (2) Each customer sending custom DPA + questionnaires (high overhead). (3) Need scalable assurance mechanism. |
| **Approach** | (1) Already had ISO 27001 (ISMS in place). (2) Extended to ISO 27701 as PII PROCESSOR (Annex B). (3) Scope: all PII processing on behalf of customers in cloud platform. (4) Timeline: 6 months from gap analysis to certification. |
| **Key controls implemented** | (1) B.8.2: Customer agreement templates (standard DPA covering 27701 requirements). (2) B.8.4: PII retention and disposal (automated; customer-controlled). (3) B.8.5: Sub-processor management (registry; notification; due diligence). (4) Clause 6: Privacy-enhanced access controls; encryption; logging. |
| **Certification outcome** | ISO 27701 certificate as PII PROCESSOR. Published on trust center. Customers accept certificate instead of custom questionnaires. |
| **ROI** | Before: 300+ hours/year responding to customer privacy questionnaires. After: certificate answers 80% of questions. Time saved: 240+ hours/year. New business: won 3 enterprise deals where ISO 27701 was procurement requirement. |

### 9.2 Healthcare Organization: Controller Certification

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Organization** | Hospital group; 5 hospitals; 10,000 employees; processes patient data (special category Art. 9) |
| **Regulatory context** | GDPR (EU); national health data regulations; patient right to access medical records |
| **Implementation challenges** | (1) Complex PII inventory (300+ systems; EHR; imaging; labs; admin). (2) Many legal bases (consent; vital interests; legal obligation; public health). (3) Special category data requires heightened controls. (4) Staff turnover → training challenge. |
| **Approach** | (1) Extended existing ISO 27001 to 27701 (controller). (2) Comprehensive data mapping (6-month project with department liaisons). (3) DPIA for all major systems (identified 8 high-risk processing activities). (4) Patient rights portal (access; portability in FHIR format). (5) Training: mandatory annual privacy training; role-based (clinicians; admin; IT). |
| **Key metrics** | DSR response time: median 12 days (target: 30). Privacy incidents: reduced 40% year-over-year. Training completion: 97%. DPIA coverage: 100% of high-risk activities. |
| **Certification** | Achieved ISO 27001 + ISO 27701 (controller). Used as evidence for national DPA inspection (no findings). Patient trust improved (communicated certification in patient communications). |

## Chapter 11 — Interview Questions & Career Guide

### Tier 1: Entry-Level

**Q1:** What is ISO 27701 and how does it relate to ISO 27001?

**A:**

| Aspect | ISO 27001 | ISO 27701 |
|:------:|:---------:|:---------:|
| **Focus** | Information SECURITY management | PRIVACY (PII) management |
| **Type** | Management system (certifiable) | EXTENSION to 27001 for privacy (certifiable) |
| **Relationship** | Foundation (standalone) | Builds ON TOP of 27001 (cannot exist without it) |
| **Controls** | 93 security controls (27002:2022) | + Privacy extensions (Clause 6) + Controller controls (Annex A: 31) + Processor controls (Annex B: 18) |
| **Risk focus** | Risks to information (CIA triad: organization perspective) | Risks to PII PRINCIPALS (impact on individuals) |
| **Certification** | ISO 27001 certificate | ISO 27701 certificate (requires ISO 27001 as prerequisite) |

**Key insight:** ISO 27701 does NOT replace 27001 — it extends it. You need BOTH. Think of it as: security is necessary for privacy, but not sufficient.

### Tier 2: Mid-Level

**Q2:** Explain how privacy risk assessment in ISO 27701 differs from information security risk assessment in ISO 27001.

**A:**

| Dimension | ISO 27001 Risk Assessment | ISO 27701 Privacy Risk Assessment |
|:---------:|:---:|:---:|
| **Whose risk?** | Risk to the ORGANIZATION (financial loss; reputation; operational disruption) | Risk to PII PRINCIPALS (individuals whose data is processed) |
| **Impact assessment** | Impact on business (revenue loss; regulatory fines; competitive disadvantage) | Impact on INDIVIDUALS (discrimination; identity theft; financial harm; reputational damage; physical harm) |
| **Assets** | Information assets (databases; documents; systems) | PII and processing activities (the data AND what you do with it) |
| **Threats** | Security threats (hackers; malware; insider threats; disasters) | Privacy threats (unauthorized collection; excessive retention; re-identification; function creep; unlawful surveillance) |
| **Vulnerabilities** | Technical/organizational weaknesses | Privacy weaknesses (lack of consent; excessive data collection; inadequate anonymization; insufficient access controls) |
| **Example risk** | "Database breach leads to €1M fine and reputation damage" | "Unauthorized access to health data leads to discrimination against patients in employment decisions" |
| **Treatment** | Security controls (encryption; firewalls; access controls) | Privacy controls (data minimization; purpose limitation; consent management; pseudonymization) + security controls |

### Tier 3: Senior

**Q3:** You are implementing ISO 27701 for a multinational acting as both controller AND processor. Design the scope and control applicability approach.

**A:**

**Scope definition:**

| Component | Detail |
|:---------:|--------|
| **Controller activities** | HR data (employees globally); customer data (marketing; CRM; support); website visitors (analytics; cookies) |
| **Processor activities** | Processing data on behalf of enterprise clients (SaaS platform); hosting; data analytics services |
| **Geographic scope** | EU (GDPR); US (CCPA/CPRA); Brazil (LGPD); Japan (APPI) |
| **PIMS boundary** | All PII processing within corporate IT systems + cloud infrastructure + SaaS platform |

**Control applicability:**

| Control Set | Applicability | Justification |
|:---:|:---:|---|
| Clause 5 (PIMS requirements) | ALL applicable | Extends 27001 management system for all PII processing |
| Clause 6 (PIMS guidance) | ALL applicable | Privacy-enhanced controls for all information security measures |
| Annex A (Controller controls) | APPLICABLE for: HR data; customer data; website visitors | Organization is controller for these processing activities |
| Annex B (Processor controls) | APPLICABLE for: SaaS platform processing | Organization is processor on behalf of clients |
| Annex A controls NOT applicable to processor activities | EXCLUDED (documented in SoA) | For SaaS: clients determine purpose/means; we follow instructions |
| Annex B controls NOT applicable to controller activities | EXCLUDED (documented in SoA) | For HR/customer: we determine purpose; no "customer" directing processing |

**Statement of Applicability (SoA) approach:**
- Single SoA covering both roles
- Each control marked: Applicable (Controller); Applicable (Processor); Applicable (Both); Not Applicable (with justification)
- Separate procedures where needed (e.g., consent management = controller only; sub-processor management = processor only)
