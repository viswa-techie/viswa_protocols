HISTORICAL CONTEXT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1969 — PLC invented (Dick Morley, Modicon 084): programmable logic replaces relay logic
1984 — CIM (Computer Integrated Manufacturing): first concept of digital factory
1994 — ISA-88 Batch Control standard published
2000 — OPC Classic (COM-based) widely deployed in industrial SCADA
2002 — Festo/SAP/Siemens begin Industry 4.0 concept discussions (pre-naming)
2004 — OPC UA development begins (Microsoft COM-free)
2006 — OPC UA Part 1-5 first published; IEC 62264 (ISA-95) published
2011 — "Industry 4.0" term coined at Hannover Messe (German government initiative)
2013 — German government RAMI 4.0 reference model development
2016 — RAMI 4.0 published as DIN SPEC 91345
         IIC (Industrial Internet Consortium) founded in USA
         Asset Administration Shell (Verwaltungsschale) concept published
2017 — Digital Twin Consortium (DTC) founded
         ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 41 (IoT) and SC 42 (AI) established
2019 — IEC 62890:2020 published (life cycle for industrial systems)
2021 — ISO 23247 Digital Twin for Manufacturing published (4 parts)
2022 — AAS IDTA specifications (Plattform Industrie 4.0, Germany)
2023 — SEMI E187/E188 cybersecurity for semiconductor fabs (major)
2024 — Industrial Metaverse emergence; Siemens Xcelerator, AWS IoT Twin Maker
