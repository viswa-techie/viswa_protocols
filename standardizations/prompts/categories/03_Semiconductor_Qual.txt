  MIL-STD-750F       — Test Methods for Semiconductor Devices

  DESC DLA            — Defense Logistics Agency drawing requirements

RELIABILITY METRICS:
  FIT Rate            — Failures In Time (failures per 10⁹ device hours)
  DPPM                — Defective Parts Per Million (outgoing quality)
  MTTF / MTBF         — Mean Time To/Between Failure (Arrhenius extrapolation)
  PFH                 — Probability of Failure per Hour (IEC 61508 target)
  AFR                 — Annual Failure Rate
  Arrhenius equation  — Ea (activation energy); acceleration factor for temperature
  Black's equation    — Electromigration lifetime model
  Coffin-Manson       — Thermal cycling fatigue model
  Eyring model        — Multi-stress acceleration (voltage + temperature)

ESD PROTECTION STANDARDS:
  ANSI/ESD S20.20:2021 — ESD control program for electronics manufacturing
  IEC 61340-5-1        — ESD protection (international equivalent)
  JEDEC JESD22-A114F   — HBM ±2000V test method
  JEDEC JESD22-C101F   — CDM test method (more relevant for modern ICs)
  AEC-Q100-002         — HBM for automotive ICs (target ±2000V all pins)

FAILURE ANALYSIS:
  JEDEC JEP131         — Soft Error Testing and Rate Methodology
  SEMI G85-0312        — Failure analysis methodology
  IPC-7711/7721        — Rework, repair, modification of electronic assemblies
  FIB (Focused Ion Beam), SEM, TEM, EBIC, Photon Emission Microscopy

SILICON LIFECYCLE MANAGEMENT:
  ISO 26262 Part 11   — Functional safety for semiconductors (PMHF, SPFM, LFM)
  JEDEC JEP001        — Silicon Lifecycle Management (SLM) concepts
  IEEE P2851          — Trusted chip and die attestation

HISTORICAL TIMELINE:
  1947 — Transistor invented (Bell Labs)
  1962 — JEDEC founded (as EIA-JC, then JEDEC Solid State)
  1975 — AEC (Automotive Electronics Council) preliminary work
  1994 — AEC-Q100 Rev A (first automotive IC qualification standard)
  1999 — AEC-Q200 (passive components); Pb-free solder transition begins
  2001 — AEC-Q100 Rev C; JEDEC DDR SDRAM standards
  2006 — AEC-Q100 Rev F; RoHS compliance AEC-Q001
  2010 — AEC-Q102 (optoelectronics); FinFET effects on qualification
  2014 — AEC-Q103 (MEMS qualification)
  2019 — AEC-Q104 (MCM); sub-10nm qualification challenges
  2022 — SEMI E187/188 fab equipment cyber; advanced packaging qualification gaps
  2023 — Chiplet qualification gaps (no AEC-Q for die-to-die): active gap
  2024 — AEC-Q100 Rev H; JEDEC HBM3E; qualification for 2nm/3nm nodes
