ROOT STANDARD:
  IEC 61508:2010 (Ed.2)        — Functional Safety of E/E/PE Safety-Related Systems
      Part 1: General requirements (scope, definitions, management)
      Part 2: Requirements for E/E/PE safety-related systems (hardware)
      Part 3: Software requirements (development, V&V)
      Part 4: Definitions and abbreviations
      Part 5: Examples of methods for determination of SIL
      Part 6: Guidelines on application of IEC 61508-2 and -3
      Part 7: Overview of techniques and measures
      SIL 1 (PFH < 10⁻⁵/hr) to SIL 4 (PFH < 10⁻⁹/hr)
      Key concepts: safety function, safety integrity level, safety lifecycle

AUTOMOTIVE (IEC 61508 domain-specific):
  ISO 26262:2018 (2nd Ed.)     — Road vehicles: Functional safety
      Part 1: Vocabulary
      Part 2: Management of functional safety
      Part 3: Concept phase
      Part 4: Product development at system level
      Part 5: Product development at hardware level (hardware metrics)
      Part 6: Product development at software level
      Part 7: Production, operation, service, decommissioning
      Part 8: Supporting processes
      Part 9: ASIL decomposition
      Part 10: Guideline on ISO 26262
      Part 11: Guidelines on application to semiconductors
      Part 12: Adaptation for motorcycles
      ASIL A/B/C/D (D = highest integrity); QM (no safety requirement)
  ISO 21448:2022               — SOTIF (Safety of The Intended Functionality)

AVIONICS (Independent domain):
  DO-178C (RTCA/EUROCAE ED-12C) — Software Considerations in Airborne Systems
      DAL A (catastrophic) through DAL E (no effect)
      Objectives: 71 objectives for DAL A; MC/DC coverage
  DO-254 (RTCA/EUROCAE ED-80)  — Design Assurance Guidance for Airborne Electronic Hardware
  DO-331                        — Model-Based Development & Verification supplement
  DO-332                        — Object-Oriented Technology and Related Techniques supplement
  DO-333                        — Formal Methods supplement
  DO-326A / ED-202A             — Airworthiness Security Process Specification
  DO-356A / ED-203A             — Airworthiness Security Methods and Considerations

SYSTEMS LEVEL (AVIONICS):
  ARP4754A (SAE)               — Guidelines for Development of Civil Aircraft and Systems
  ARP4761A (SAE)               — Safety Assessment Methods (FHA, PSSA, SSA)

MEDICAL (IEC 61508 domain-specific):
  IEC 62304:2006+AMD1:2015     — Medical device software lifecycle processes
      Software safety classes: A (no injury), B (non-serious injury), C (serious injury/death)
  IEC 82304-1:2016             — Health software (standalone software)
  IEC 60601-1:2005+AMD1+AMD2   — Medical electrical equipment (electrical safety + FuSa)

RAILWAY (IEC 61508 domain-specific):
  EN 50128:2011+AMD1:2020      — Software for railway control systems (SIL 0-4)
  EN 50129:2018                — Safety-related electronic systems for signaling
  EN 50126-1:2017              — RAMS for railway applications

MACHINERY (IEC 61508 domain-specific):
  IEC 62061:2021+AMD1          — Safety of machinery: SIL assignment
  EN ISO 13849-1:2023          — Safety of machinery: Performance Level required (PLr)
  EN ISO 13849-2               — Validation of safety-related control systems

PROCESS INDUSTRY:
  IEC 61511:2016               — Functional safety for process industry (SIS systems)
      Relates to IEC 61508; Process Industry Sector Standard

ANALYSIS METHODS:
  FMEA: Failure Mode and Effects Analysis
  FMEDA: FMEA + Diagnostics Analysis (IEC 61508 hardware architecture)
  FTA: Fault Tree Analysis (top-down: probability of hazardous event)
  HAZOP: Hazard and Operability Study (process, guide words)
  ETA: Event Tree Analysis (forward from initiating event)
  STAMP/STPA: Systems-theoretic methods (Nancy Leveson, MIT)
  Common Cause Failure (CCF): β-factor method (IEC 61508)

FAMOUS INCIDENTS THAT SHAPED FUNCTIONAL SAFETY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

For each: system, failure, root cause, what standard now prevents it:
  Therac-25 (1985-87)          — Race condition, removed hardware safety → IEC 62304
  Bhopal (1984)                — Process safety failure → IEC 61511
  Piper Alpha (1988)           — Offshore platform fire, 167 dead → IEC 61511
  Challenger (1986)            — O-ring management, SRM → NASA system safety (DO-178C)
  Ariane 5 (1996)              — Software reuse without re-validation → DO-178C §12
  Patriot Missile (1991)       — Floating point accumulation error → software safety
  Toyota Unintended Accel.     — MISRA violations, stack overflow → ISO 26262
  Boeing 737 MAX (2018/2019)   — MCAS single sensor, inadequate FMEA → ARP4754A/DO-178C
  Viareggio (2009)             — Rail axle fatigue → EN 50126 RAMS
