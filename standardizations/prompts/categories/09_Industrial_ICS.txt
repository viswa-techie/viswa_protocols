HISTORICAL TIMELINE:
  1975 — Modbus invented by Modicon (Dick Morley)
  1977 — PROFIBUS development begins (Germany)
  1989 — IEC 61131 begins; first PLCs commercialized (1968 Modicon 084)
  1993 — PROFIBUS IEC 61784; international fieldbus wars
  1994 — Foundation Fieldbus; fieldbus wars (PROFIBUS vs. FF vs. ControlNet)
  1996 — IEC 61131-3 published (PLC programming languages)
  1999 — EtherNet/IP (ODVA); industrial Ethernet begins
  2001 — OPC Classic (OPC DA) widely adopted
  2003 — EtherCAT (Beckhoff) launched
  2005 — OPC Unified Architecture (OPC UA) design begins
  2006 — Stuxnet (discovered 2010) development: SCADA attack proof-of-concept
  2008 — ISA-99 → IEC 62443 formal adoption process begins
  2010 — Stuxnet discovered: ICS security emergency
  2014 — IEC 62443-4-2 drafts; SHODAN exposes 100K+ internet-connected PLCs
  2016 — Industroyer/CrashOverride: Ukraine grid attack (second attack)
  2017 — TRITON/TRISIS: SIS attack (Schneider Triconex); IEC 62443 SL4 urgency
  2020 — OPC UA FX (Field Exchange) for publisher-subscriber
  2023 — Volt Typhoon (China): critical infrastructure pre-positioning
  2024 — IEC 62443-2-1 revision; IT/OT convergence security gaps
