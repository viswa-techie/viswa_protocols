SUBSTATION AUTOMATION & PROTECTION:
  IEC 61850:2013               — Communication networks for power utility automation
      Part 6: SCL (Substation Configuration Language)
      Part 7-1: Principles and models
      Part 7-3: Common data classes
      Part 7-4: Compatible logical node classes and data classes
      Part 8-1: MMS and XMPP (SCADA communication mapping)
      Part 9-2: Sampled Values (process bus, merging units)
      Part 90-12: Wide-area network to IEC 61968/61970 (smart grid gateway)
  IEC 60255                    — Measuring relays and protection equipment
  IEC 61968                    — Application integration at electric utilities (CIM)
  IEC 61970                    — Energy management system API (CIM-based)
  IEC 60870-5                  — Telecontrol equipment and systems (legacy SCADA)
      IEC 60870-5-101/103/104  — Specific companion standards
  DNP3 (IEEE 1815-2012)        — Distributed Network Protocol (US utilities)

HISTORICAL CONTEXT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1882 — Edison's Pearl Street Station (NYC): first central power grid
1889 — Edison-Westinghouse AC/DC war; Niagara Falls hydroelectric plant (AC wins)
1907 — First interconnected power grids (UK, Germany)
1965 — Northeast Blackout (USA/Canada): 30M people, 13 hours → triggered grid reliability work
1968 — NERC (North American Electric Reliability Corporation) founded
1977 — New York City Blackout: looting, civil unrest → grid investment increase
1984 — IEC SC57 formed: utility communication standards
1996 — Western US cascading blackout: 12 states, 2M customers
2000 — IEC 61850 Part 1-2 published (first edition)
2003 — Northeast Blackout (USA/Canada): 50M people, 55B USD loss
         → NERC CIP mandatory standard process begins
2003 — Ukrainian power grid attack: first proven cyberattack on power grid (Slammer worm)
2005 — NERC CIP standards development accelerated
2007 — Aurora Generator Test (Idaho): cyber command destroys generator via protection relay
2009 — Smart Metering rollout begins Europe (EU mandate for 80% by 2020)
2010 — Stuxnet: ICS cyberattack paradigm shift (nuclear relevance)
2015/2016 — Ukraine BlackEnergy/Industroyer: 225,000 customers lost power
              → NERC CIP supply chain (CIP-013) development
2018 — IEC 61850 Edition 2.1 (enhanced cybersecurity edition)
2019 — First NERC CIP enforcement penalty >$10M (US utility)
2021 — Colonial Pipeline ransomware: fuel supply disruption → ICS security emergency
2022 — Sandworm Industroyer2 attack on Ukraine grid
         Texas grid failure (Winter Storm Uri): 246 dead, $195B damage
2023 — IEEE 1547-2018 grid interconnection for solar/wind/battery widely adopted
2024 — EU NIS2 makes energy grid operators subject to strict cybersecurity requirements
