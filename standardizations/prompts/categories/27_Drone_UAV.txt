1916 — Kettering Bug: US WWI aerial torpedo (first modern UAV)
1935 — DH.82B Queen Bee: First full-scale reusable radio-controlled drone (UK)
1950s — Firebee target drones (Ryan AQM-34) for Korean/Vietnam reconnaissance
1973 — Israel uses Scout/Mastiff drones for Yom Kippur War reconnaissance
1994 — General Atomics Predator first flight (later armed with Hellfire)
2001 — Post-9/11: Predator/Reaper deployment in Afghanistan; mass military UAS use
2010 — DJI Phantom 1 (2013): consumer drone revolution; millions sold
2012 — FAA Modernization and Reform Act: first mandate for UAS integration (Public Law 112-95)
2016 — FAA Part 107: first commercial small UAS rule (USA)
         Registration required for >250g drones
2019 — EU 2019/947 and 2019/945: Open/Specific/Certified categories
         DJI Mavic Mini (<250g) designed specifically to avoid EU registration
2020 — COVID: Zipline drones deliver medical supplies in Rwanda/Ghana at scale
2021 — Amazon Prime Air Part 135 Air Carrier certification granted
         FAA Remote ID final rule published
2022 — Joby/Archer/Lilium eVTOL programs advance type certification
         FAA P-8110-13 criteria published for first batch of eVTOLs
2023 — Remote ID enforcement begins (USA); EASA U-Space regulation
2024 — FAA Part 108 (proposed): BVLOS operations framework
         Drone warfare in Ukraine: EW, GPS spoofing, FPV kamikaze drones dominate tactics
2025 — Joby Aviation FAA certification expected; multiple eVTOL commercial operations
         China CAAC type certificates for consumer drones
2026 — Urban Air Mobility (UAM) first commercial passenger operations expected

CRITICAL CONCEPTS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. JARUS SORA v2.5 Methodology:
   Ground Risk Class (GRC): 1-10 based on operation over people density
   Air Risk Class (ARC): a-d based on airspace type
   Specific Assurance & Integrity Level (SAIL): I-VI
   Operational Safety Objectives (OSOs): 24 OSOs with integrity levels

2. EASA Category Definitions:
   Open (C0-C3): Low risk, no authorization needed
   Specific (PDRA, STS, Authorized): SORA required
   Certified: Full airworthiness (passengers, hazmat, large UAS over people)

3. FAA BVLOS Pathways:
   Part 107 waiver process
   Part 135 Air Carrier (for package delivery)
   UAS Integration Pilot Program (UIPP) results
   BEYOND Act (proposed legislation for BVLOS normalization)

4. eVTOL Certification Basis:
   FAA: 14 CFR Part 21.17(b) Special Class + Issue Papers
   EASA: SC-VTOL + AMC/GM
   Powered Lift category: bridges helicopter and fixed-wing regulations
   Key challenges: propulsion redundancy, battery failure, fly-by-wire certification

5. Remote ID:
   Broadcast RID: Wi-Fi / Bluetooth beacon from drone
   Network RID: cloud-based tracking
   USS (UAS Service Supplier) ecosystem
