STANDARDS UNIVERSE:
TRANSPORT SAFETY (Lithium Batteries):
  UN Manual of Tests and Criteria Section 38.3 (UN 38.3, Rev.7)
      Tests: altitude simulation, thermal, vibration, shock, short circuit, impact, overcharge
      All lithium batteries must pass before transport
  IATA DGR (Dangerous Goods Regulations)  — Li battery air transport restrictions
  ICAO Technical Instructions             — Civil aviation Li battery rules
  US DOT 49 CFR Part 173.185             — US ground transport requirements
  IMDG Code                              — Maritime transport of dangerous goods

  UL 1642 (2012)               — Lithium batteries (safety tests for individual cells)
  UL 2054 (2011)               — Household and commercial batteries (pack level)
  IEC 60086-4                  — Primary lithium batteries: safety requirements

HISTORICAL TIMELINE:
  1991 — Sony: first commercial Li-ion battery (18650 cell)
  1994 — UL 1642 first edition; Li-ion transport safety rules begin
  1998 — UN 38.3 first edition for Li battery transport
  2006 — Sony laptop battery recall (Dell/Apple): thermal runaway awareness
  2013 — Boeing 787 battery fire: EASA/FAA Li-ion avionics certification challenge
  2016 — Samsung Galaxy Note 7: 100 aircraft battery fires → IEC 62133-2 revision
  2017 — IEC 62619: industrial Li-ion standard published
  2019 — ISO 6469 series updated for modern EVs
  2022 — UL 9540A thermal propagation testing; NFPA 855 stationary storage
  2023 — SAE J3400 (NACS): Tesla standard adopted by US automakers
  2024 — IEC 62619:2022 widely adopted; EU Battery Regulation 2023/1542 (passport)
  2025 — EU Battery Regulation: recycled content requirements; digital battery passport
