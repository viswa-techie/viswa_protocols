PLATFORM INTEROPERABILITY:
  Khronos OpenXR 1.1           — Cross-platform XR API (runtime + application layer)
      Layers: API layer, runtime, OS-specific back-end
      Supported: Android (Meta, Pico), Windows (SteamVR, WMR), Linux
  Khronos OpenGL ES 3.2        — Mobile/embedded graphics
  Khronos Vulkan 1.3           — High-performance cross-platform graphics
  Khronos OpenCL 3.0           — Compute for XR processing
  Khronos glTF 2.0             — 3D asset transmission format (WebXR, AR)
  Khronos WebGL 2.0            — Web-based 3D graphics (underpins WebXR)
  W3C WebXR Device API         — Browser-based XR (AR/VR in web)
  W3C WebXR Anchors, Hit Test, DOM Overlay modules

DISPLAY & VISUAL STANDARDS:
  VESA DisplayPort 2.1         — High-bandwidth display for headsets
  HDMI 2.1+                    — Headset tethered display interface
  MIPI DSI-2                   — Panel interface for HMD displays
  DisplayHDR (VESA)            — HDR certification for XR displays
  IEEE 2048                    — Resolution/frame rate requirements (draft)
  Foveated Rendering specs     — Vendor-specific (eye tracking + variable resolution)

SAFETY & HEALTH:
  IEC 62471:2006               — Photobiological safety (eye safety: lasers, LEDs in HMDs)
  IEC 62471-5:2015             — Scanning laser safety (LiDAR in XR)
  EN ISO 10819                 — Vibration transmitted through hand (haptic safety)
  IEEE P2048 series            — XR health and safety standards (developing)
  FCC OET Bulletin 65          — RF exposure for tethered wireless headsets
  SAR limits (ICNIRP/FCC)      — Head-mounted device RF safety

ENTERPRISE & INDUSTRIAL XR:
  ISO 11442 (Technical documentation) — AR assembly instructions standard
  IEEE P1589 (Augmented Reality) — AR learning technology standard
  IEC 62676 (Video surveillance) — AR-enhanced surveillance systems
  MIL-STD-1472G               — Human factors engineering (applies to AR HMDs in defense)
  OSHA 1910 (Industrial Safety) — XR training qualification recognition

ACCESSIBILITY:
  W3C WCAG 2.1 / 3.0           — Web content accessibility (applies to WebXR)
  XR Access Initiative         — Accessibility in XR best practices
  ISO/IEC 40500:2012           — WCAG 2.0 as ISO standard

PRIVACY & DATA:
  GDPR + biometric XR data     — Eye tracking, facial expression = biometric data
  ISO/IEC 27701                — Privacy information management (XR platform operators)
  ETSI TS 103 485              — XR devices: privacy and security requirements (draft)

HISTORICAL CONTEXT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1968 — Ivan Sutherland: "Sword of Damocles" — first head-mounted display
1991 — Jaron Lanier coins "Virtual Reality"; VPL Research DataGlove
1995 — Nintendo Virtual Boy: commercial failure; motion sickness concerns raise safety
1999 — Khronos Group founded (OpenGL oversight, future XR standards)
2012 — Oculus Rift Kickstarter ($2.4M raised): modern VR renaissance
2015 — Microsoft HoloLens (Gen 1): enterprise AR; Google Glass failure analysis
2016 — Pokemon GO: AR mass market; 500M downloads
         Oculus Rift CV1 + HTC Vive commercial VR launch
         Khronos begins OpenXR development (fragmentation problem)
2019 — Khronos OpenXR 0.90 preliminary spec
2020 — Khronos OpenXR 1.0: major platform alignment (Valve, Epic, Microsoft, Facebook)
2021 — Facebook rebrands to Meta; $10B+ metaverse investment announced
2022 — Meta Quest Pro, PSVR2; enterprise XR (Toyota, Boeing, Lockheed)
         W3C WebXR becomes W3C Recommendation
2023 — Apple Vision Pro announced (spatial computing); OpenXR 1.0 stable
         IEEE P2048 XR health/safety working group accelerates
2024 — Apple Vision Pro ships; enterprise industrial XR at scale
         XR biometric data (eye tracking) triggers GDPR scrutiny
         Generative AI + XR convergence
2025 — Android XR (Google + Samsung); OpenXR 1.2 draft; W3C WebXR 2.0
         Industrial metaverse standards (ISO/IEC, Digital Twin Consortium)
