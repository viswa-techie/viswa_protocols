STANDARDS UNIVERSE:
HAZARDOUS SUBSTANCE RESTRICTIONS:
  RoHS 3 Directive (EU 2015/863/EU) — Restriction of Hazardous Substances (10 substances)
      8 from RoHS 2: Pb, Hg, Cd, Cr(VI), PBB, PBDE, DEHP, BBP, DBP, DIBP
      Applies to EEE placed on EU market; max 0.1% (0.01% Cd) by weight
  REACH Regulation (EC 1907/2006)  — Registration, Evaluation, Authorisation and Restriction
      SVHC (Substances of Very High Concern): Article 59 candidate list (250+ substances)
      SVHC >0.1% in article: inform customers; >1 tonne/year: ECHA notification
      Authorisation: PFAS, phthalates, hexavalent chromium
  WEEE Directive (2012/19/EU)      — Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment
      Producer responsibility; collection targets (45/65%); recycling categories
  RoHS China (SJ/T 11364-2014)     — China RoHS marking requirements
  RoHS Japan (JPOSSM)               — Japan Green Procurement Guidelines
  TSCA (US EPA)                     — Toxic Substances Control Act (Frank R. Lautenberg Chemical Safety Act 2016)
  California Prop 65               — OEHHA listed chemicals; discharge warning

PFAS & EMERGING POLLUTANTS:
  REACH PFAS restriction (Annex XV) — EU universal PFAS restriction proposal (pending 2026)
  US PFAS Strategic Roadmap (EPA)  — Drinking water, cleanup
  EU Drinking Water Directive        — 0.1 μg/L total PFAS limit
  ECHA PFAS classification           — C8/C4 PFOS/PFOA/PFNA already restricted under Stockholm

CONFLICT MINERALS:
  US Dodd-Frank Act Section 1502    — 3TG minerals (tantalum, tin, tungsten, gold) from DRC
  OECD Due Diligence Guidance       — Responsible mineral supply chains
  EU Conflict Minerals Regulation (2017/821) — Effective Jan 2021
  RMI (Responsible Minerals Initiative) — CMRT (Conflict Minerals Reporting Template)
  London Bullion Market Association  — Responsible Gold Guidance

ENVIRONMENTAL MANAGEMENT SYSTEMS:
  ISO 14001:2015                    — Environmental Management System (EMS)
  ISO 14004:2016                    — EMS: guidance for implementation
  ISO 14040:2006                    — Life Cycle Assessment (LCA): principles
  ISO 14044:2006                    — LCA: requirements and guidelines
  ISO 14064 series                  — GHG inventory and verification
  ISO 50001:2018                    — Energy Management System (EnMS)

GREENHOUSE GAS & CLIMATE:
  GHG Protocol (WRI/WBCSD)         — Corporate accounting and reporting standard
      Scope 1 (direct); Scope 2 (purchased energy); Scope 3 (value chain)
  Paris Agreement (2015)            — 1.5°C target; NDC reporting
  TCFD (Task Force on Climate-Related Financial Disclosures) — Climate risk disclosure
  ISSB IFRS S1/S2 (2023)           — Sustainability disclosure standards (replaces TCFD)
  EU CSRD (Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive 2022) — Mandatory ESG reporting
  EU Taxonomy Regulation (2020/852) — Green finance classification
  SBTI (Science Based Targets initiative) — Corporate emissions reduction pathways
  SEC Climate Disclosure Rules (2024) — Scope 1/2 emissions in financial filings

ELECTRONIC RECYCLING & CIRCULAR ECONOMY:
  EU Ecodesign Regulation (ESPR 2022) — Right to repair, recyclability, digital product passport
  EU Battery Regulation 2023/1542   — Battery passport, recycled content targets, extended producer responsibility
  IPC-1752A                         — Materials Declaration (replaces IPC-1752)
  IPC-4101C                         — Specification for Base Materials (halogen-free)
  JEDEC JESD97                      — Marking and Labeling of Lead-Free
  IPC-9701A                         — Performance Test Methods for Pb-Free Solder
  IEC 62321 series                  — RoHS substance testing methods

ESG REPORTING FRAMEWORKS:

  SASB Standards                    — Industry-specific ESG metrics (now ISSB)
  CDP (Carbon Disclosure Project)   — Supply chain climate questionnaire
  ISO 26000:2010                    — Guidance on social responsibility
  B Corp Certification              — Social/environmental performance standards
  UN SDGs (Sustainable Development Goals) — 17 goals, 169 targets

HISTORICAL TIMELINE:
  1972 — Stockholm Conference on Human Environment (UN first env. conference)
  1987 — Montreal Protocol: CFCs and ozone
  1994 — Basel Convention: transboundary hazardous waste
  1995 — EU Packaging Directive
  2001 — Stockholm Convention: POPs (persistent organic pollutants)
  2003 — EU RoHS 2002/95/EC published; WEEE 2002/96/EC
  2006 — RoHS enforcement; REACH regulation enters into force
  2011 — RoHS 2 (2011/65/EU) + WEEE 2012 update
  2012 — WEEE Directive recast
  2015 — RoHS 3 delegated regulation for 4 phthalates
  2016 — Paris Agreement enters into force
  2020 — TCFD recommendations widely adopted by financial regulators
  2021 — EU Conflict Minerals Regulation full enforcement
  2022 — EU CSRD; EU Battery Regulation; ISSB formation
  2023 — ISSB IFRS S1/S2 published; US SEC climate rules proposed
  2024 — EU ESPR digital product passport; PFAS universal restriction proposal
