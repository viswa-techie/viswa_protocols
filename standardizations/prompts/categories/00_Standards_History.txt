01_Pre_1970_Industrial_Origins.md
    1901 — BSI (British Standards Institution) founded (first national standards body)
    1906 — IEC (International Electrotechnical Commission) founded
    1912 — SOLAS 1914 (Titanic disaster triggered)
    1919 — ICAN/ICAO predecessor (post-WWI aviation regulation)
    1926 — ISA (ISA = then International Federation of the National Standardizing Associations)
    1932 — IEC first electromagnetic compatibility discussions
    1947 — ISO founded (post-WWII: 25 national bodies, Geneva)
    1948 — IMO (then IMCO) founded
    1950s — US MIL-SPEC explosion (Korean War, Cold War defense electronics)
    1954 — IEC TC65 (industrial process measurement & control) formed
    1957 — Sputnik: space standards development begins
    1960 — SOLAS 1960 (updated post-WWII)
    1963 — US FCC established (Radio Act heritage, 1934)
    1964 — ASCII standard (ANSI X3.4): first widely adopted digital standard
    1965 — IEEE formed (merger of AIEE + IRE)
    1968 — NERC founded (Northeast Blackout 1965 trigger)
    1969 — ARPANET: origin of internet protocols (IETF standards later)

02_1970s_1980s_Safety_Awakening.md
    1972 — OSHA established (US Occupational Safety and Health Act 1970)
    1974 — SOLAS 1974 (comprehensive current framework)
    1975 — IEEE 802 committee formed (LAN standards)
    1976 — Seveso disaster (Italy): toxic chemical cloud → Seveso Directive (EU chemical safety)
    1977 — IEC TC56 (Dependability) established
    1979 — Three Mile Island: nuclear safety standards (NRC rules)
             Robert Williams killed by industrial robot → OSHA robot guidance
             IEC 60364 (building electrical installation) published
    1982 — MIL-STD-882B (System Safety): established hazard risk methodology
    1983 — TCP/IP becomes ARPANET standard: internet protocols foundation
    1984 — Bhopal disaster (3,787 dead): chemical process safety IEC 61511 origin
    1985 — Therac-25 accidents (1985-1987): software safety → IEC 62304, IEC 61508
    1986 — Chernobyl: IAEA nuclear safety standards accelerated
             Challenger disaster: NASA system safety culture reform
    1987 — VHDL IEEE 1076: first formal HDL standard
    1988 — Clapham Junction rail crash: EN 50128 origin
    1989 — IEC TC65A WG9: IEC 61508 development begins

03_1990s_Digital_Era_Standards.md
    1990 — Motorola Six Sigma (process quality: ISO 9001 impact)
    1991 — WWW invented (Tim Berners-Lee): W3C standards era begins
    1993 — W3C founded (Tim Berners-Lee)
    1994 — IEC 61508 Working Draft: functional safety root standard
             AUTOSAR predecessor discussions (Bosch/BMW/DaimlerChrysler/Volkswagen)
    1995 — IEEE 802.11 Wi-Fi standard v1: wireless era
             Verilog IEEE 1364-1995
    1996 — FIDO Alliance precursor cryptographic work
             IEC 61850 TC57 WG10 starts (utility automation)
    1997 — ISO 14001:1996 (environmental management system)
    1998 — Internet security awareness: CERT/CC, SANS Institute established
             ISO/IEC 17799 (information security; became ISO 27001)
    1999 — Ariane 5 Flight 501 (1996 actually): software reuse safety lesson (documented 1999)
             IEC 61508:1998 published (first edition)
             Y2K: first global software compliance program (date format standardization)
    2000 — IEC 61850 Part 1-2 published (utility automation)

04_2000s_Embedded_Internet_Era.md
    2001 — 9/11: ISPS Code (maritime), FAA security rules, ITAR tightening
    2002 — PCI DSS v1.0 (payment card security: Visa/MC/Amex/JCB/Discover)
             Sarbanes-Oxley: IT audit controls for financial systems
    2003 — NERC CIP development begins (Northeast blackout)
             ISO 26262 development begins (based on IEC 61508)
    2004 — AUTOSAR founding (BMW, Bosch, Continental, DaimlerChrysler, Siemens VDO, VW)
             ISO/IEC 27001 first edition (ISMS)
    2005 — IEEE 1800-2005 SystemVerilog standardized
             NIST SP 800-53 v1 (federal cybersecurity)
    2006 — AUTOSAR R1.0 released; EU Machinery Directive 2006/42/EC
             3GPP Release 8 (LTE foundations)
    2007 — NERC CIP v1 standards published
             iPhone: mobile security/app standards era begins
    2008 — Heartland breach: PCI DSS emergency revision
             FIPS 140-2 widely adopted for cryptographic modules
    2009 — OPC UA IEC 62541 published (industrial comms)
             ISO 26262 CD (Committee Draft)

05_2010s_Connected_Vehicle_Cloud.md
    2010 — Stuxnet: first nation-state ICS cyberattack; IEC 62443 urgency
             iOS App Store security model: mobile app store standards influence
    2011 — ISO 26262:2011 first edition (automotive functional safety)
             UVM 1.0 (Accellera): verification methodology unification
             AUTOSAR R4.0: major architecture
    2012 — NIST Cybersecurity Framework development begins
    2013 — FIDO Alliance founded: passwordless auth standards
             ISO/SAE 21434 concept work begins
    2014 — NIST CSF v1.0 published
             Heartbleed (OpenSSL): supply chain vulnerability awareness
             ISO/IEC 27018: cloud PII protection
    2015 — Jeep Cherokee remote hack (Miller/Valasek): vehicle cybersecurity emergency
             → ISO/SAE 21434 accelerated; UNECE R155/R156 development
             US EMV liability shift; chip card standardization push
    2016 — NIST SP 800-63-3: digital identity levels
             EU GDPR published (enforcement 2018)
             PSD2: Open Banking mandate (EU)
             3GPP Release 15: 5G NR standards complete
    2017 — WannaCry ransomware: global OT/IT supply chain damage
             ISO/IEC 27017 cloud security; Cloud Act (US)
             ISO/TS 15066 collaborative robot safety
    2018 — EU GDPR enforcement begins
             NIST PQC competition Round 1 submissions
             ISO 26262:2018 (second edition, major revision)
             AUTOSAR Adaptive Platform R18-10
    2019 — 3GPP 5G NR commercial deployments (Korea, US, China, Germany)
             NIST AI framework discussions begin
             MIL-STD-810H published (environmental)
