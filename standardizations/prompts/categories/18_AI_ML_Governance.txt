STANDARDS UNIVERSE:
EU ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE ACT:
  EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689) — Published August 2024; phased enforcement
      Risk tiers:
        UNACCEPTABLE: banned AI (social scoring, real-time biometrics public)
        HIGH RISK: medical, safety, infrastructure, HR, law enforcement
        LIMITED RISK: chatbots, deep fakes (transparency obligations)
        MINIMAL RISK: spam filters, AI in games
      High-risk AI obligations:
        Conformity assessment (Annex VII: notified body or self-assessment)
        Quality management system (ISO 42001 aligned)
        Technical documentation, logs, human oversight, accuracy/robustness/cybersecurity
        Registration in EU AI database
      GPAI (General Purpose AI) rules: systemic risk, copyright, transparency
      Penalties: €30M or 6% global turnover
      Timeline: Prohibited AI — Feb 2025; GPAI — Aug 2025; High-risk — Aug 2026

HISTORICAL TIMELINE:
  1950 — Alan Turing: "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" (Turing Test)
  1956 — Dartmouth Conference: "Artificial Intelligence" term coined
  1986 — Backpropagation (Rumelhart, Hinton): neural network training
  1997 — Deep Blue beats Kasparov: AI in specialized domains
  2012 — AlexNet: deep learning revolution (ImageNet competition)
  2016 — AlphaGo: reinforcement learning at world champion level
  2017 — Transformer architecture (Vaswani: "Attention is All You Need")
  2018 — BERT (Google): language model pre-training
  2019 — GPT-2 (OpenAI): responsible disclosure debate
  2020 — GPT-3: 175B parameters; OECD AI Observatory
  2021 — EU AI Act proposal; NIST AI RMF v1.0 draft
  2022 — ChatGPT (Nov 2022): public AI governance crisis begins
  2023 — GPT-4; Claude 2; EU AI Act political agreement; NIST AI RMF 1.0
  2024 — EU AI Act published (Aug 2024); ISO 42001 certifications; NIST GenAI profile
