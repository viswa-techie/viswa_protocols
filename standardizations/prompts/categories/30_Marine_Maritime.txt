IMO (International Maritime Organization):
  SOLAS (Safety of Life at Sea) — Consolidated Edition 2020
      Chapter II-1: Construction, structure, subdivision
      Chapter II-2: Fire protection, detection, extinction
      Chapter III: Life-saving appliances
      Chapter IV: Radiocommunications (GMDSS)
      Chapter V: Safety of navigation (ECDIS, AIS, VDR, LRIT)
      Chapter XI-2: Special measures to enhance maritime security (ISPS Code)
  MARPOL (Marine Pollution) — Annexes I-VI
      Annex VI: Air pollution (SOx, NOx, GHG)
  ISM Code (International Safety Management) — Safety management systems
  ISPS Code — International Ship & Port Facility Security Code
  STCW Convention — Standards of Training, Certification and Watchkeeping
  MLC (Maritime Labour Convention) 2006 — Seafarer working conditions
  COLREGS — Convention on the International Regulations for Preventing Collisions at Sea
  UNCLOS — UN Convention on the Law of the Sea
  Load Line Convention (LL66/88) — Structural integrity and freeboard

  IEC 62287-1:2017             — AIS (Automatic Identification System) Class A

  IEC 60092 series             — Electrical installations in ships
  IEC 60533:2015               — Electrical and electronic installations (EMC)
  IEC 61191-2                  — Electronic assemblies (printed board assembly)

HISTORICAL CONTEXT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1912 — RMS Titanic sinking: 1,500 dead → triggers first SOLAS Convention (1914)
1929 — SOLAS 1929: international agreement on minimum safety standards
1948 — IMO established (as IMCO, Inter-Governmental Maritime Consultative Organization)
1960 — SOLAS 1960: major update post-WWII maritime expansion
1974 — SOLAS 1974 (current base): comprehensive safety framework
1978 — SOLAS Protocol 1978: tanker safety post-Amoco Cadiz spill
1979 — Amoco Cadiz: 1.6M barrels spilled → MARPOL ratification acceleration
1989 — Exxon Valdez: 37,000 tons crude → OPA 90 (USA) and MARPOL Annex I tightening
1994 — Estonia ferry disaster (852 dead): roll-on/roll-off ferry stability requirements
2001 — 9/11 → ISPS Code development for port and ship security
2004 — ISM Code becomes universal for all ships >500 GT
2012 — Costa Concordia (32 dead): evacuation procedures, ECDIS mandatory timeline set
2016 — ECDIS mandatory for most vessel types (finally)
2017 — IMO MSC-FAL.1/Circ.3: first maritime cyber guidance
2019 — IMO 2020: global 0.5% sulphur cap (SOx regulations, MARPOL Annex VI)
2021 — IMO initial GHG strategy: 50% reduction by 2050
2022 — IACS UR E26/E27: mandatory cyber resilience requirements for new ships
2023 — IMO revised GHG strategy: net-zero by 2050
         MASS Code development (autonomous ships regulatory framework)
2024 — Maritime cyber incidents increase (GPS spoofing in Black Sea, Red Sea)
