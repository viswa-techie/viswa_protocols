HISTORICAL CONTEXT TIMELINE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1938 — US War Department begins standardizing military specifications
1949 — MIL-SPEC system formally established post-WWII (military standardization reform)
1962 — Cuban Missile Crisis triggers classified EM hardening requirements
1980 — MIL-STD-2167 established (software development for defense systems)
1986 — MIL-STD-461D published (EMI/EMC for defense equipment)
1992 — ITAR regulations strengthened post-Cold War export control reform
1994 — DoD moves to Commercial Off-The-Shelf (COTS) preference — Perry Reform
1996 — MIL-STD-882D (system safety) — evolution from industrial accidents
2001 — DoD RDT&E paradigm shift post-9/11; ITAR enforcement intensified
2008 — Counterfeit parts crisis (Chinese counterfeit ICs in military aircraft)
         → SAE AS6081, AS5553 counterfeit avoidance standards
2012 — MIL-STD-882E (current system safety standard)
2014 — FACE Technical Standard v2.0 (avionics software portability)
2016 — DoDI 8510.01 RMF replaces DIACAP for cybersecurity
2018 — National Defense Authorization Act bans Huawei/ZTE in federal supply chain
2019 — MIL-STD-810H (current environmental standard)
2020 — NDAA Section 889: Supply chain security requirements formalized
2022 — Ukraine conflict highlights EW vulnerability, GPS jamming, drone warfare
2023 — CMMC 2.0 (Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification) finalized
2024 — MOSA mandate for all new DoD program starts
2025 — AI in defense: DoD AI Ethics Principles enforcement; autonomous weapons debate
