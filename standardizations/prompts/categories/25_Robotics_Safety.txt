HISTORICAL CONTEXT TIMELINE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1921 — Karel Čapek coins "robot" in play R.U.R.
1954 — George Devol patents Unimate (first programmable robot)
1961 — Unimate deployed at GM Ewing, NJ — first industrial robot
1979 — Robert Williams killed by industrial robot arm at Ford Flat Rock plant
         → First robot-related fatality → triggered OSHA investigations
1984 — OSHA issues robot safety guidelines; RIA establishes R15.06
1987 — ISO TC 184/SC2 established for robot standards
1989 — ANSI/RIA R15.06-1986 (first formal robot safety standard)
1999 — ISO 10218 first published (industrial robots)
2006 — EU Machinery Directive 2006/42/EC replaces 98/37/EC
2011 — ISO 10218-1:2011 and ISO 10218-2:2011 (current major revision)
         Universal Robots launches UR5 (first commercial cobot)
2016 — ISO/TS 15066:2016 (collaborative robot safety — power & force limits)
         Key incident: Worker arm crushed when TS 15066 not applied correctly
2021 — ISO 13482 revision; IEC 62061:2021 edition 3 (machinery SIL)
2023 — EU Machinery Regulation 2023/1230 (replaces Machinery Directive by 2027)
         EU AI Act classifies certain robots as "high-risk AI"
2024 — ISO 10218-1:2025 (draft) major revision with AMR/cobot updates
         ROS 2 Safety WG publishes first safety profiles
2025 — ISO/TS 15066 revision (new biomechanical data, higher payloads)
