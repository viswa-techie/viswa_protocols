STANDARDS UNIVERSE:
PRIMARY VEHICLE CYBERSECURITY STANDARDS:
  ISO/SAE 21434:2021           — Road Vehicles: Cybersecurity Engineering
      Defines CSMS (Cybersecurity Management System)
      Clause 9: Distributed cybersecurity activities (supply chain)
      Clause 10: Concept phase (item definition, TARA)
      Clause 11: Product development
      Clause 12: Production
      Clause 13: Post-development (monitoring, response, updates)
      Clause 14: End of life
      Appendix B: TARA methods
      Appendix C: CAL (Cybersecurity Assurance Level) table

  UNECE WP.29 R155 (2021)      — Cybersecurity Requirements for Vehicles (Type Approval)
      Mandatory: Cyber Security Management System (CSMS) approval by UN type approval authority
      Scope: vehicles M, N, O, L categories with ECUs
      Enforcement: new types July 2022; all new vehicles July 2024 (EU)

  UNECE WP.29 R156 (2021)      — Software Update Requirements (SOTA/FOTA)
      Mandatory: Software Update Management System (SUMS) approval
      Ensures OTA updates do not compromise safety or security
      Applies to vehicles with software-updateable systems

  SAE J3101                    — Hardware Protected Security Environments for Ground Vehicles
  SAE J3061                    — Cybersecurity Guidebook for Cyber-Physical Vehicle Systems (2016, pre-21434)

THREAT ANALYSIS AND RISK ASSESSMENT (TARA):
  ISO/SAE 21434 Clause 15      — TARA methodology
  STRIDE threat modeling       — Spoofing, Tampering, Repudiation, Info Disclosure, DoS, Elevation
  EVITA project                — E-safety Vehicle Intrusion proTected Applications
  HEAVENS project              — HEAling Vulnerabilities to ENhance Software Security and Safety
  Damage scenarios → impact rating → risk value → cybersecurity goals
  CAL 1-4 (Cybersecurity Assurance Level) — analogous to ASIL in ISO 26262
  Attack feasibility: elapsed time, specialist expertise, knowledge, window of opportunity

KEY INCIDENTS:

  2016 — Tesla Autopilot fatality: OTA update response; Model S CAN bus research
  2022 — Rivian, Ford, Toyota debug port vulnerabilities
  2023 — Marc Rogers: charging station OCPP attack → EV charging security gap

HISTORICAL TIMELINE:

  2016 — SAE J3061: first vehicle cyber guidebook; NHTSA guidance
  2017 — UNECE WP.29 IWG on CS/OTA begins
  2020 — ISO/SAE 21434 CD published; US EO 14028 awareness
  2021 — ISO/SAE 21434:2021 published; UNECE R155/R156 adopted
  2022 — UNECE R155 mandatory (new types); AutoISAC guidelines updated
  2024 — UNECE R155 mandatory (all new vehicles EU); China GB/T equivalent
