STANDARDS UNIVERSE:
US REGULATORY:
  FCC Part 15                   — Radio Frequency Devices (unintentional/intentional radiators)
      Subpart B: Unintentional radiators (Class A industrial, Class B residential)
      Subpart C: Intentional radiators (Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, ZigBee)
      Subpart D: Unlicensed personal communications services
  FCC Part 68                   — Connection of terminal equipment to telephone network
  FCC CFR 47 Part 25            — Satellite equipment
  SAR (Specific Absorption Rate) — FCC OET Bulletin 65: 1.6 W/kg (US)
                                   ICNIRP: 2.0 W/kg (EU); averaged over 10g tissue
  FCC ID authorization           — 3 paths: DoC, SDoC, Certification by TCB
  UL 62368-1 (2019)             — Audio/video, IT, and communication safety
  UL 1310                        — Class 2 power supplies
  ANSI/UL 2054                   — Household and commercial batteries

EU REGULATORY (CE MARKING):
  Radio Equipment Directive (RED 2014/53/EU) — Replaces R&TTE directive
      Essential requirements: §3.1(a) safety, §3.1(b) EMC, §3.2 spectrum efficiency
      Delegated acts: §3.3(d) privacy, §3.3(e) network protection, §3.3(f) anti-fraud
      RED §3.3 delayed mandate: August 2025 enforcement for IoT/phones/tablets
  Low Voltage Directive (LVD 2014/35/EU) — 50V-1000V AC, 75V-1500V DC
  EMC Directive (2014/30/EU)    — Emissions and immunity for non-radio equipment
  Machinery Directive (2006/42/EC) — Safety of industrial machines
  Machinery Regulation 2023/1230 — Replaces 2006/42/EC (fully in force 2027)
  RoHS Directive (2011/65/EU)   — Hazardous substance restrictions (see Cat 14)
  Ecodesign Regulation 2019/2020 — Energy efficiency requirements
  Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR 2022) — Updated product sustainability

INTERNATIONAL SAFETY STANDARDS:
  IEC 62368-1:2018              — Audio/Video/IT/Comms equipment (replaced UL 60950/IEC 60065)
      Hazard-Based Safety Engineering (HBSE) methodology
      Source → transfer → body → severity levels
  IEC 60950-1                   — IT equipment safety (superseded by 62368-1, widely used)
  IEC 60335 series              — Household and similar electrical appliances
  IEC 60065                     — Audio/video appliances (superseded by 62368-1)
  IEC 60825-1 (2014)            — Laser safety; Class 1 to Class 4
  IEC 62471 (2006)              — Photobiological safety of lamps

EMC STANDARDS:
  CISPR 32 (2012)               — Multimedia equipment: emission limits
  CISPR 35 (2016)               — Multimedia equipment: immunity
  CISPR 22 (superseded)         — IT equipment emissions
  CISPR 24 (superseded)         — IT equipment immunity
  IEC 61000 series:
      IEC 61000-3-2              — Harmonic current limits
      IEC 61000-3-3              — Voltage fluctuation / flicker
      IEC 61000-4-2              — ESD immunity
      IEC 61000-4-3              — Radiated immunity (GTEM)
      IEC 61000-4-4              — EFT/Burst immunity
      IEC 61000-4-5              — Surge immunity
      IEC 61000-4-6              — Conducted disturbances immunity
  ICES-003 (Canada)             — Digital apparatus emissions
  AS/NZS CISPR 32               — Australia/NZ equivalent

JAPAN:
  VCCI                          — Voluntary Control Council for Interference (IT equipment)
  MIC (Ministry of Internal Affairs) — Certification for radio equipment
  PSE (Act on Product Safety for Consumer Products) — Electrical appliances
  PAL/TELEC                     — Telecom equipment certification

KOREA:
  NRA (National Radio Agency) / MSIT — Radio certification (EMC + RF)
  KC Mark                       — Mandatory product safety for Korean market
  KCC (Korea Communications Commission) — Equipment registration

INDIA:
  BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards) — Compulsory registration order (CRO) for electronics
  WPC (Wireless Planning & Coordination) — Type approval for RF devices
  TRAI                          — Telecom spectrum regulatory

CHINA:
  SRRC (State Radio Regulation Committee) — Radio type approval
  MIIT (Ministry of Industry and IT) — Telecom terminal equipment
  3C (China Compulsory Certification) — CCC for safety, EMC, telecom
  GB/T standards                — National equivalents to IEC standards

  IEC 62619:2017                — Secondary cells for industrial use

HISTORICAL TIMELINE:
  1934 — FCC established (Communications Act 1934)
  1968 — FCC Part 15 first issue
  1985 — FCC Part 15 Class A/B radiated/conducted limits
  1992 — CE marking (EEC Directives consolidated)
  1995 — RoHS lead-free solder directives begin (EU)
  1996 — FCC 1.6 W/kg SAR limit for mobile phones
  1999 — ICNIRP EMF guidelines (2.0 W/kg)
  2002 — RoHS 2002/95/EC published (effective 2006)
  2004 — R&TTE Directive for radio equipment (EU)
  2011 — RoHS 2 (2011/65/EU); 62368-1 project begins
  2014 — RED replaces R&TTE; LVD 2014/35/EU
  2016 — FCC IoT security guidance (post-Mirai)
  2019 — IEC 62368-1:2018 (3rd edition); replaces 60950-1 and 60065
  2022 — EU Radio Equipment Directive §3.3 cybersecurity delegated acts
  2024 — EU RED §3.3 IoT cybersecurity enforcement begins
  2025 — BIS India expanded CRO list; China 3C for IoT
