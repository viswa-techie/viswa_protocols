3GPP RELEASE FAMILY:
  Release 8 (2009)              — LTE (Long Term Evolution) foundation: OFDMA, MIMO
  Release 9 (2010)              — LTE-Advanced prep, eMBMS, SON
  Release 10 (2011)             — LTE-Advanced: carrier aggregation, eICIC
  Release 11 (2013)             — CoMP, enhanced PDCCH
  Release 12 (2015)             — D2D (device-to-device), small cells
  Release 13 (2016)             — LTE-Advanced Pro, IoT (NB-IoT, eMTC, EC-GSM-IoT)
  Release 14 (2017)             — V2X (C-V2X Sidelink), FD-MIMO
  Release 15 (2019)             — 5G NR Phase 1: NSA + SA, eMBB, sub-6 GHz + mmWave
  Release 16 (2020)             — 5G Phase 2: URLLC, MIMO enhancements, V2X NR
  Release 17 (2022)             — NR-U (unlicensed), RedCap (IoT), NTN (satellite)
  Release 18 (2024)             — 5G Advanced: AI/ML in network, Ambient IoT, sidelink relay
  Release 19 (2025)             — 5G Advanced Phase 2; 6G preparation
  IMT-2030 (6G)                 — ITU-R M.2160 vision document; 3GPP starts feasibility

HISTORICAL TIMELINE:
  1979 — NTT launches world's first cellular network (1G)
  1987 — GSM standard agreed (2G)
  1991 — First GSM call (Finland)
  1998 — 3GPP formed; cdma2000 (3GPP2) formed
  2001 — 3G WCDMA (UMTS) deployments
  2004 — 3GPP Release 6; HSDPA (3.5G)
  2008 — 3GPP Release 8: LTE standardized
  2012 — LTE-Advanced (4G); VoLTE
  2015 — M2M SIM standardization (GSMA SGP.02)
  2016 — NB-IoT and eMTC for IoT
  2017 — 5G NR specification completed (3GPP R15)
  2019 — First 5G commercial deployments (Korea, US, China)
  2020 — 5G standalone networks; C-V2X emerging
  2021 — O-RAN deployments; satellite 5G (NTN)
  2022 — 5G RedCap for IoT
  2024 — 5G Advanced (R18) commercial; 6G research programs (EU Hexa-X, US NextG)
  2030 — 6G expected commercial (ITU IMT-2030)
